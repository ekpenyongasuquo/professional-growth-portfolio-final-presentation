IT 497 – Final Portfolio (Week 6 Submission)

Introduction: A Coherent Journey of Discipleship and Professional Purpose

This portfolio represents the culmination of my spiritual and professional development throughout this course. I have come to understand that my identity as a disciple of Jesus Christ directly shapes my identity as an aspiring cybersecurity and IT professional. My guiding principles—faith, integrity, discipline, stewardship, and service—now inform how I think, make decisions, and interact with others in academic and professional environments. These principles align with the doctrine that all aspects of life should be gathered together in Christ, influencing both personal character and professional conduct (Bednar, 2018).

As I reflect on my journey, I recognize that spiritual progression and professional growth are not separate paths but integrated responsibilities. My commitment to ethical technology use, data protection, and responsible system administration is an extension of my discipleship and covenant commitment to act with honesty, accountability, and love toward others (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, 2013).

Spiritual Progression Portfolio

Depth of Reflection on Spiritual Growth

Throughout this course, I experienced profound spiritual refinement as I intentionally evaluated my thoughts, decisions, and reliance on the Savior. I recognized moments where impatience, self-reliance, and performance pressure challenged my discipleship. These moments became sacred opportunities for repentance, humility, and increased dependence on divine guidance. I learned that spiritual growth is not passive; it requires consistent faith, effort, and accountability, much like climbing a personal “Mount Sinai” of refinement and covenant commitment (Hurley, n.d.).

This reflection helped me see that becoming a trusted disciple in my field means aligning my daily actions with Christlike attributes such as patience, honesty, compassion, and diligence. I now approach academic work, peer collaboration, and future employment as opportunities to represent my faith through ethical behavior and service to others (Morrison, n.d.).

Application of Spiritual Principles in Real Life

I applied spiritual principles by receiving peer feedback with humility, responding with gratitude rather than defensiveness, and revising my work to reflect higher standards of integrity and clarity. In group discussions, I practiced Christlike communication by being respectful, supportive, and solution-focused. These experiences strengthened my commitment to live gospel principles not only in worship but also in academic and professional responsibilities (Bednar, 2018).

Future Spiritual Goals as a Disciple

  • Maintain daily prayer and scripture study to seek guidance in decisions
  • Evaluate ethical dilemmas through gospel-centered principles
  • Serve and mentor others using Christlike leadership
  • Remain accountable for continuous spiritual repentance and growth

Professional Growth Portfolio

Professional Development and Ethical Responsibility

This course strengthened my understanding that technical competence must always be governed by ethical responsibility. As a future cybersecurity professional, I will handle sensitive data, system access controls, and digital infrastructure that impact real people. Viewing technology stewardship as a sacred trust helps me avoid misuse of technical skills and instead prioritize confidentiality, integrity, and responsible monitoring. This perspective aligns with gospel teachings on honesty and accountability in all professional dealings (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, 2013).

Technical Skills and Context-Based Ethical Application

Through my coursework in networking, scripting, cloud technologies, and security operations, I developed skills in system configuration, monitoring, and secure data handling. I now apply these skills with an ethical framework, ensuring that privacy, compliance, and responsible system use guide all technical decisions. For example, when analyzing logs or configuring access permissions, I view each action as impacting real users who deserve protection and trust. This ethical approach prepares me to responsibly manage user data in real-world IT environments where trust and compliance are critical (Hurley, n.d.).

Career Vision and Professional Mission

  • Develop advanced competencies in cybersecurity and cloud security
  • Uphold ethical data protection and privacy standards
  • Apply integrity, discipline, and stewardship in all technical roles
  • Create secure systems that positively impact individuals and organizations

Detailed Action Plan for Ongoing Spiritual and Professional Development

  • Set quarterly spiritual reflection and professional growth goals
  • Participate in ethical cybersecurity and compliance training
  • Seek mentorship from faith-centered and industry professionals
  • Evaluate major career decisions using gospel and ethical frameworks
  • Engage in service-oriented technology projects that benefit others

Reflective Essay: Alignment With My Goals and Their Evolution

At the beginning of this course, my goals were primarily focused on academic achievement and technical excellence. Over time, those goals evolved to include character development, ethical responsibility, and Christlike service. I now recognize that professional success without spiritual grounding is incomplete. My refined vision is to become a cybersecurity professional who not only protects systems but also blesses lives through ethical leadership and trustworthy service.

This portfolio demonstrates how my goals shifted from performance-driven ambitions to purpose-driven discipleship. I now measure success not only by technical competence but also by integrity, accountability, and the positive impact I have on others. This evolution reflects my commitment to lifelong growth as a disciple of Jesus Christ and a responsible IT professional (Bednar, 2018; Morrison, n.d.).
